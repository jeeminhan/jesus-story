Direction 1 of 8
The Receiver
The non-believer the product is ultimately for. She arrives via a shared card — she doesn't know it's a gospel app. She taps because it felt personal. She has no church background, no patience for religious language, and zero tolerance for feeling converted. The experience has to earn her trust one scene at a time.
e.g. Priya — gets a card from her friend Maya after a hard month
The Carrier
The believer who shares it. She experienced the product herself, felt something real, and wants to send it to someone specific — not as a mission, just as a gift. She knows which path fits this person. She decides how transparent the card should be. Her key moment: "Who in my life needs to hear this right now?"
e.g. Maya — sends Priya a card tuned to the grief path, Art Card style so Priya doesn't see the source yet
The Bridge
Anything that crosses between Carrier and Receiver. The scene card is a Bridge — the Carrier creates it, the Receiver sees it cold in a WhatsApp thread before she knows anything about the app. It has to look like art to the Receiver and feel meaningful to the Carrier at the exact same moment.
e.g. the scene card — an Art Card has no source attribution; a Story Card shows "John 4:29"
The Coordinator
Backstage — a ministry team member who follows up with Receivers who opted into connecting. Her UX is a simple dashboard: name, path taken, what they wrote. She needs context, not just contact info. She doesn't appear in these screens but is the reason D8 exists.
e.g. Sarah — sees "Priya · grief path · 'I didn't expect to feel this'"

Direction 4
"The Mirror"
Receiver
The Receiver's peak moment — at the end of her journey. She didn't know she was in a story. Now the story reflects her back. This screen gets screenshotted.
Grief · Post-narrative peak
Design Choices
No illustration — this moment belongs entirely to the words
Three paths, not two — each one honors a different place she might be in
"Tell me more" — her curiosity leads her to the source on her own terms. Not told; she asks.
"Talk to someone" — connects to D8. Still no pressure; she decided, nothing pushed her.
"Sit with this" — the lightest option. Smallest text. She can close the app. That's a valid ending.

Direction 6
"The Card"
Bridge
The handoff point. The Carrier sends it — the Receiver sees it first. No Bible citation on the face. No app name. No path label. Just art. The meaning reveals itself inside.
Grief · Share Surface · OG Image
Design Choices
One card format. No source attribution, ever. A believer recognizes the quote; a non-believer meets it as literature.
Upper 82% is pure illustration — no UI, no branding, no path label
Quote only at the illustration base — no "John 4:29", no chapter, no verse number
OG link previews show the illustration — no religious markers in WhatsApp or iMessage thumbnails
Bottom bar is minimal: "Thinking of someone?" + share. Not a nav bar.

Direction 7
"Who Needs This?"
Carrier
Carrier-only screen. She picks a person from her contacts, selects the emotional path that fits them, and chooses how transparent the card should be.
Post-Revelation · Carrier UX
Design Choices
Carrier picks a specific person — not a broadcast. Feels like writing a letter.
Carrier selects the emotional path that fits this person — they know her
One card format for everyone — no source shown. Believers recognize the quote; that's enough.
Send button warm gold — activation energy, not a form submission
No "back" affordance — this isn't interruptible

Direction 8
"I'm Here"
Receiver
Receiver-only. Post-mirror moment. She can connect — or step back. Both feel equally valid. No pressure. This is the last screen she sees and it must leave her feeling respected either way.
Grief · Post-Mirror · Connect Prompt
Design Choices
Sarah's face and name shown — a real person, not a system. Trust through specificity.
"She'll read what you write and reach out if you want her to" — she confirms the opt-in
"No agenda. She won't try to convince you of anything." — said plainly, not in small print
CTA: "Send my story" — not "submit" or "connect." She's sharing something, not filling out a form.
"Not yet" same visual weight as send — closing the app is a complete, valid ending

Direction 9
"I Was There Too"
Receiver
Post-mirror witness moment. Reached via "Tell me more." A real person — same emotional path — looks at camera and says: I was where you are.
Grief · Post-Mirror · Witness
Design Choices
Short vertical video, 15–20 seconds. Raw, not produced. Filmed like a voice memo — personal.
One person per emotional path — grief gets a grief story, not a general testimonial
Name visible, no last name. Brief context: what path they took, nothing religious.
Their words shown below the video as a single pull quote — she can read it without sound
Same three paths below — now heavier, because she's seen a real person carry them
